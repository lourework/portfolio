### Merinio — Employee Experience Redesign

**Role:** UX/UI Designer
**Product:** Workforce Management SaaS
**Platform:** Web App
**Duration:** 2023–2024
**Focus:** Employee-facing experience

Merinio is a SaaS platform that helps companies manage employee scheduling, time tracking, and communication.
In this project, I focused on improving the **employee-facing experience**, redesigning key areas of the Employee Portal to make daily tasks clearer, faster, and more intuitive.
My work concentrated on simplifying complex workflows while modernizing the interface to support scalability and long-term product growth.

#### The Problem
As the product evolved, the Employee Portal accumulated features that were essential but increasingly difficult to navigate. Employees struggled to quickly understand their schedules, approve shifts, and communicate efficiently with managers.
This complexity impacted usability, clarity, and overall adoption of the platform.

#### Goals
- Improve clarity and usability of employee workflows
- Reduce friction in scheduling and shift approval
- Modernize the interface while respecting existing system constraints
- Create a more intuitive and consistent experience across features

#### My Role
I was responsible for:
- UX/UI redesign of employee-facing flows
- Layout modernization and visual hierarchy
- Improving information architecture and interactions
- Collaborating with developers to ensure feasible solutions

#### Process
- Analyzing existing flows
- Identifying friction points
- Redesigning layouts and interactions
- Iterating with feedback from the team
- Aligning UI decisions with business and technical constraints

#### Key Solutions

**Employee Portal Redesign**
I redesigned the Employee Portal interface, focusing on clarity and ease of use.
The new layout improved navigation, visual hierarchy, and readability, helping employees quickly access schedules, availability, and actions without friction.

**Schedule & Shift Management**
I improved the shift management interface to make viewing, approving, and understanding shifts more intuitive.
The redesigned flow reduced cognitive load and made critical actions easier to identify and complete.

**Internal Chat Experience**
I restructured the internal chat system, redesigning conversations, contact details, groups, message flow, and attachments.
The goal was to create a clearer communication experience between employees and managers, aligned with the rest of the product's UI.

**Geolocation for Punch Records**
I introduced a map-based feature to employee punch records, allowing managers to verify where punch-in and punch-out actions were made.
This increased transparency and trust, ensuring employees were registering hours from the correct location via the Employee Portal web app.

#### Impact
- Improved clarity of daily employee tasks
- Reduced friction in shift approval and scheduling
- More consistent and modern UI across employee features
- Stronger alignment between user needs and business rules

#### Learnings
This project reinforced the importance of designing scalable systems, balancing usability with technical constraints, and improving complex workflows without overwhelming the user.

### Merinio — Automation & Bulk Management Features

**Role:** UX/UI Designer
**Product:** Workforce Management SaaS
**Platform:** Web App
**Duration:** 2023–2024
**Focus:** Automation & productivity

This case focuses on features designed to **optimize repetitive administrative tasks**, reducing manual effort and improving efficiency for managers working with large volumes of data.

#### The Problem
Managers were required to apply changes one item at a time — whether adjusting wage premiums or editing punch cards and projects.
This manual process was time-consuming, error-prone, and inefficient at scale.

#### Goals
- Reduce repetitive manual actions
- Enable bulk operations across multiple items
- Improve efficiency and accuracy
- Support scalable workforce management

#### My Role
I was responsible for:
- UX/UI design for automation flows
- Improving complex form interactions
- Optimizing UI for bulk actions
- Collaborating closely with developers on constraints

#### Key Solutions

**Wage Premiums Automation**
I redesigned the wage premium creation flow, enabling automated application of premiums to shifts and punch records.
This reduced manual configuration and made complex rules easier to understand and manage.

**Bulk Editions — Punch Cards & Projects**
I optimized the UI to allow bulk editing of multiple shifts, punch cards, and projects at once.
This change significantly reduced the time managers spent managing payroll-related data, replacing one-by-one edits with efficient bulk actions.

#### Impact
- Significant reduction in repetitive manual work
- Faster management of shifts and payroll data
- Improved efficiency for operational teams
- Better scalability for growing organizations

#### Learnings
Designing automation features requires balancing power and simplicity. Clear UI patterns and well-structured flows are essential when users manage complex data at scale.

### Motto Mortgage — Office Locator

**Tags:** UX/UI · Web · Location-based Search

**Motto Mortgage** is a mortgage lending company with a nationwide network of offices across the United States. Its website supports users in finding nearby offices and accessing essential information during the decision-making process.

#### The Challenge
The office search experience relied on a list-based interface that required users to leave the page to view locations on a map, creating unnecessary friction and breaking the navigation flow. The challenge was to improve usability and clarity when searching for office addresses, making location information easier to understand, faster to access, and more context-aware.

#### My Role
While working as a **UX/UI Designer at Matilha Estúdio**, I was responsible for designing a solution to improve the office search experience.
My contribution included:
- Integrating an interactive map directly into the search page
- Displaying office locations with map pins for better spatial understanding
- Enhancing the results list with real-time office status based on operating hours
- Improving the overall flow to reduce page exits and support faster decision-making

#### Outcome
The redesigned experience connected list and map views in a single interface, allowing users to quickly locate offices, understand their availability, and access relevant information without leaving the page. The solution reduced friction in the search process and delivered a clearer, more intuitive location experience.

### COZY — Remote Psychological Care | Case Study

## Facilitating remote therapy.

In my first UX Design study, I accepted the challenge of understanding how to take advantage of telemedicine opportunities to improve the health of Brazilians who need psychological or psychiatric help, creating a solution focused on care and monitoring without geographic barriers.

### How to take advantage of telemedicine opportunities to improve the health of Brazilians who need psychological or psychiatric help.

#### The Current Scenario
The approval of online psychological care took place in November 2019 by the CRP (Regional Psychology Council). By April 2020, a month after the Covid-19 pandemic began, Google searches for online therapy had increased by more than 88%.

With the situation of social isolation due to the pandemic, the safest alternative for patients who chose not to interrupt treatment is through online care, which for them can also be more convenient, economical and less time consuming. For the psychologist, the economy factor is also a positive point since, for many, it is not necessary to maintain a service room, but he must pay attention to good means of communication.

Feelings and emotions to live a worldwide pandemic boosted the increase in searches for an online psychologist, even people who have never done therapy, in this case the difficulty that can be found is the lack of confidence in professional and service mode, which deviates from the traditional.

#### Project's Goal
Create an online psychological care platform that connects psychologists with patients, facilitating care and follow-up, so that Brazilians can be assisted remotely.

---

#### Users
The product is aimed at two user profiles: patients and psychologists. To better visualize these profiles, I created two proto-personas and their journeys, which through qualitative and quantitative research were validated and updated.

#### Personas

**User 1 — Patient (Julia Maria)**

Journey Map story:
> *Once upon a time* Julia Maria, 24 years old, a publicist who lives alone in Curitiba because her parents live in the countryside. Due to the pandemic, she started working remotely, which messed up her routine, as she works more than her usual schedule.
> *Every day* wakes up early, turns on your computer, prepares your coffee and then sits at your desk and starts checking emails and the demands of the day. She usually orders food from apps, has lunch in front of her computer, which makes her tense, without the necessary breaks.
> *One day*, while working, Julia noticed her hands trembling and tingling, she also began to notice that her breathing was not normal. He Googled what he was feeling and among so many possibilities, he saw that they could be symptoms of anxiety disorder.
> *Because of this*, he started looking for content about anxiety on Instagram and as he consumed the content, he realized the benefits that therapy would bring to his life. He then decided to look for a platform where he could be served remotely, that would meet his payment needs and facilitate his choice of a professional.
> *After* your first appointment, the professional who assists you identifies the symptoms, makes your diagnosis correctly and guides you about the changes and adaptations you could make to improve your quality of life with the home office.
> *Until he finally* noticed his improvement in anxiety crises, he felt lighter and more confident with his mental health being treated by a qualified professional, even inside his home.

**User 2 — Psychologist (Letícia)**

Journey Map story:
> *Once upon a time*, Letícia, a psychologist, married, practices Yoga at home and attended to her patients from Monday to Friday, 08 hours a day, in a rented clinic in the city center until the arrival of Covid-19.
> *Every day*, Letícia wakes up very early, makes her coffee and goes to her guest room, which has been adapted into an office, for her first appointment. Between one service and another, it answers its customers who request an appointment or change of time and organizes its financial statement.
> *One day*, she realizes the need for a platform that helps her to control her schedule, her finances and that also facilitates the payment of her patients, since she couldn't offer many options for serving remotely.
> *Because of this*, Letícia began to look for platforms that facilitate remote service, meet all of her needs and transmit her confidence. She found the platform, saw all the facilities she could bring to her daily professional life, and also realized the convenience for her patients.
> *Until* finally she migrated all her patients to the new platform, got new patients and managed to reduce her bureaucratic work having more free time to invest with herself.

---

#### Validating Supposition
After defining the proto-personas and their user journeys, through the knowledge x impact prioritization method, I established a CSD Matrix with my main assumptions and doubts regarding potential users.

#### Quantitative Research

**Survey with the general public (patients)**
Of the 40 people who responded to the survey aimed at the general public, 62% do or have already undergone therapy and 37.5% said they never did.

For people who have never had therapy, 40% of the answers were because they didn't feel the need, however, 46% of the people answered that they think psychological care is too expensive.

Regarding the service, 86.7% think they would be more comfortable with face-to-face service than with online service.

**Research with psychologists**
I got answers from 6 psychologists. All respondents currently attend online, but only 50% have attended before the context of social isolation. And 50% will continue only with online care.

As for the cost, the interviewees reported that the amount charged for the consultation remained the same, thus leaving their gain a little higher than with face-to-face assistance.

#### Qualitative Research
With the answers from the quantitative survey I conducted a qualitative survey with 7 people, where my greatest lessons were:
- Some patients said they felt more comfortable having their first consultation remotely than in person.
- Psychologists' biggest frustrations are with connection stability.
- Psychologists feel that patient compliance, punctuality, and progress is remotely surprisingly good.
- The secrecy of the remote consultation is a concern for psychologists.
- The most used means of dissemination is through indication.

---

#### Solution Alternatives
Using the technique of "How could we", I listed the opportunities found:
- How could we guide the professional and facilitate the process of their online care?
- How could we convey confidence to patients and future patients so that they feel comfortable with choosing online therapy?
- How could we facilitate payment for appointments?
- How could we take the psychologist off the job of charging his defaulting patients?
- How could we solve the problem of confidentiality of consultations between patients and psychologists?

#### Solution
At this point I chose to prioritize the problem of confidentiality of consultations between patients and psychologists and the solution I offer for this problem is through an application called **Cozy**.

Through this application it will be possible to find an expert based on the answers to a questionnaire right when the user creates his account. During the consultation, made through video call, it will also be possible to send text messages, thinking of moments when the patient is not in a suitable place to carry out the consultation and is insecure to report a problem aloud.

#### Low-fidelity Prototype
Once the solutions have been defined, I created Cozy sketches using the Crazy 8's technique, then moved to low-fidelity prototype.

#### Wireframes
I applied the improvement solutions discovered with the scribble frame tests in the medium fidelity prototype (wireframe), defining and improving the flow.

#### User Tests
I ran tests with 3 users and found the following problems:
- The form where the person selects their needs lacks a confirmation button.
- In the expert list, the times placed in the slide form are confusing for the user.
- On the expert page, users expected to see the date calendar before the expert description section.

# COZY

The name Cozy comes from English and means welcoming, and that is the purpose of the application, to bring welcome to those who need psychological care.

#### Branding
The construction of the logo is done with lowercase letters and the symbol with a conversation balloon with a smile, to convey the welcome described in the name itself.
The orange color was chosen because it is a color that transmits: joy, energy and warmth; the same sensations that a warm hug can convey.

#### Styleguide
Working only with orange and its variations and shades of gray, light and dark, to make the application sophisticated.
The chosen font was Nunito, a sans serif font-family, with rounded edges that speak to the chosen identity.

### EVA | UX Design Bootcamp | Study Case

*Project under development | How Bootcamps UX Design Bootcamp, in partnership with Ella Campos, Ibraim Bernardo and Luiza Ribeiro.*

**The prominence of minorities in the labour market** was the central theme and basis for the entire journey. The Double Diamond method was used for development.

#### Quantitative Research
We distributed the survey to our personal networks. We obtained 98 respondents in 3 days. Among these 98, 61 were women who scored their pain and problems in a way that made us choose them as our audience.

**Key paths in the survey:**
- **Looking for a job:** People described problems of prejudice on the part of companies, and insecurities about their qualifications, behaviour and appearance.
- **I'm working:** We focused on knowing about the company's culture and especially if the person felt comfortable expressing their identity and visions in the place where they work.

#### Qualitative Research
We talked to two women — one currently looking for a job and another who held a leadership position and now works with women's support organizations.

#### Card Sorting & Top Insights
We categorized all our certainties and insights taken from the interviews. We managed to complete the first stage of the diamond by identifying a central problem: **insecurity**; and our audience: **Women in Technology**.

#### How Might We…?
**How to work on the insecurities of women in the area of technology to support them towards protagonism in the labour market?**

#### Benchmarking
Analysis of all female empowerment services within and outside the technology area. One interesting insight: few of them offer an app and/or an open community.

#### Brainstorming → Birth of EVA
After Crazy 8's and Free Brainstorming, the team arrived at the solution:

**EVA: Digital solution with the aim of empowering women and supporting them towards protagonism in the labor market.**

#### Personas
- **Betta**, 26 years old, who is working but seeks to reposition herself in the job market
- **Logana**, 22, who is looking for her first job

#### User's Journey
We defined the journeys of the user from the moment they find information on the platform on Social Networks, until the moment they start to receive feedback in their publication in the community.

#### Prioritization (MoSCoW Method)
Our solution was huge — we used the MoSCoW method to define the MVP.

### Banco Itaú — Mobile App Home Redesign

#### Context
This product design case was developed during a **Product Design bootcamp**, based on the analysis of real customer feedback collected from complaint platforms and app store reviews. The goal was to identify usability issues within Banco Itaú's digital experience and propose a high-impact, feasible improvement.

#### Problem
Multiple user complaints revealed friction across the banking experience, including service delays, high fees, and fragmented apps. Within the mobile app, a recurring issue stood out: the **home screen organization**.

The existing home prioritized loans and investments, while frequently used services and shortcuts were hidden or required additional navigation. This structure increased cognitive load and made everyday tasks less efficient.

#### Process
To better understand and prioritize user pain points:
- Customer complaints were analyzed and grouped using an **affinity diagram**
- Issues were evaluated through a **prioritization matrix**, balancing user impact and implementation effort
- The home screen organization emerged as a **low-effort, high-impact** opportunity

#### Solution
A redesigned home screen was proposed with:
- Customizable highlighted shortcuts based on user preferences
- Core services distributed more evenly across the screen
- Reduced need for scrolling and hidden navigation

The solution aimed to improve clarity, accessibility, and speed in everyday interactions.

#### Learnings
- Small structural changes can have a significant impact on perceived usability
- Prioritization frameworks help focus design efforts on realistic, high-value improvements
- Designing for financial products requires balancing business priorities with everyday user needs
